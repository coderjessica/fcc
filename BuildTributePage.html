The History of Pink Floyd


All 5 members of Pink Floyd, during the short time they were together. Starting at the bottom then clockwise: Waters, Barrett, Gilmour, Wright, Mason

A Time Line of the Band Pink Floyd:

1965

Pink Floyd Sound is created after a couple of years of bandmates coming and going.
The band consisted of Bob Klose and Roger Waters both on guitars, Nick Mason was the drummer, Rick Wright played the keyboard and the lead vocalist was Chris Dennis. Before the group's first single, Klose left and Dennis was replaced by Syd Barrett.
"Arnold Layne" was recorded but not released.

1966

The group is picked up by Peter Jenner and Andrew King as procuders, and they dropped "Sound" from their name.

1967

The group signed on with EMI and received an advance of 5,000 EU dollars, equivilent to almost 100,000 today.
"Arnold Layne" and later, "See Emily Play" are released.
The Piper at the Gates of Dawn is released and reaches #6 on the UK album chart.
Barrett has a mental break down and is using LSD. He stops responding during interviews and even live shows. This led to his replacement by David Gilmour in December.

1969

Ummagumma is released.
Pink Floyd began departing from their earlier works. Their 1969 album peaked at #5, spending 21 weeks on the UK album chart.

1970

Atom Heart Mother is released.
Pink Floyd has their first #1 album, it spent 18 weeks on the UK album chart.

1971

Meddle is released.

1973

Dark Side of the Moon is released.

1975

Wish You Were Here is released.

1977

Animals is released.

1979

The Wall is released.

1983

The Final Cut is released.

1994

The Division Bell is released.

2014

The Endless River is released.
