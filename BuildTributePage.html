The History of Pink Floyd

A Time Line of the Band Pink Floyd:

1965

Pink Floyd Sound is created after a couple of years of bandmates coming and going.
The band consisted of Bob Klose and Roger Waters both on guitars, Nick Mason was the drummer, Rick Wright played the keyboard and the lead vocalist was Chris Dennis. Before the group's first single, Klose left and Dennis was replaced by Syd Barrett.
"Arnold Layne" was recorded but not released.

1966

The group is picked up by Peter Jenner and Andrew King as producers, and they dropped "Sound" from their name, becoming "Pink Floyd".

1967

The group signed on with EMI and received an advance of 5,000 EU dollars, equivilent to almost 100,000 today.
"Arnold Layne" and later, "See Emily Play" are released.
The Piper at the Gates of Dawn is released and reaches #6, spending 14 weeks on the UK album chart.
Barrett has a mental break down and is using LSD. He stops responding during interviews and even live shows. This led to his replacement by David Gilmour in December.

1969

Ummagumma is released.
Pink Floyd began departing from their earlier works. Their 1969 album peaked at #5, spending 21 weeks on the UK album chart.

1970

Atom Heart Mother is released.
Pink Floyd has their first #1 album, it spent 18 weeks on the UK album chart.

1971

Meddle is released. The LP peaked at #3 and spent 82 weeks on the UK album chart.
Includes a 23 minute song.

1973

Dark Side of the Moon is released.
Waters is the sole author of the lyrics.
The album is one of the most commercially successfull rock albums of all time; a US #1.
It remained on the Billboard chart for nearly 20 years, still holding the record of the longest running Billboard album as of 2021.
This album is the world's third best-selling album, and the 21st best-selling album of all time in the US.

1975

Wish You Were Here is released.
Their last album had left Pink Floyd emotionally drained and their new album was composed purely by Gilmour, which reminded Waters of Barrett.
Pink Floyd was working on the album when Barrett randomly visited. Although he talked for a bit, he wasn't really there. The band didn't recognize him initially.
This album reached #1 in both the US and the UK.

1977

Animals is released.
Recorded in 1976 at 35 Britanna Row, Animals was a concept loosely based on George Orwell's Animal Farm.
There were many issues between band members at this time, in addition to their own personal problems. Gilmour and Waters disagreed about royalties, Wright had marital problems and issues with Waters' ego.
Gilmour also felt as if they had accomplished the success they sought after, and had nothing left to achieve.
Their 1977 album peaked on the UK chart at #2 and the US chart at #3.

1979

The Wall is released.
Waters presented two ideas for Pink Floyd's next album. One was eventually The Wall while the other ended up as Waters' first solo album.
The album was based on the story of a character called Pink who was inspired by Waters' childhood, most notably his father's death. The first "brick" led to more problems, enter drugs and depression revolving around the music industry. Pink would transform into a megalomaniac, partially revolving around the decline of Barrett. Eventually Pink would tear down the wall and become a normal person again.
During the recording of this album, the band was not impressed with the little effort Wright was putting towards the band and was kicked out.
The album topped the Billboard chart in the US for 15 weeks and reached #3 in the UK.

1983

The Final Cut is released.
The lyrics for this 1983 album were written solely by Waters, who also did all of the music. It was considered, in part, to be Waters solo album.
After The Final Cut, the band had a meeting in 1984 to discuss the future of Pink Floyd. Waters was doing a solo tour for his own album, Wright (kicked from the band), had formed another band, and Mason released his second solo album.
Waters left the meeting with the understanding that Pink Floyd was done while the other two left thinking they would resume once Waters was done with his solo tour.
Once the miscommunication became apparent, legal battles ensued over the band name.

1994

The Division Bell is released.
Gilmour, Mason and Wright worked together to create this 1994 album, and it reached #1 in both the US and UK, spending 51 weeks on the UK album chart.

2014

The Endless River is released.
Gilmour and Mason, without Waters, revisited recordings taken during The Division Bell sessions. Mason said the album was a tribute to Wright.
It became the most pre-ordered album of all time on Amazon UK and the fastest-selling in 2014.

Where are they now?

Syd Barrett was the first of the band members to pass, dying in his home in Cambridge at the age of 60 in 2006.
Rick Wright passed in 2008 from cancer at the age of 65.
Nick Mason formed a new band in 2018 to perform Pink Floyd's early material.
Roger Waters released his first solo album in 25 years in 2017 and even joined Mason on stage in 2019.
David Gilmour still writes and performs music as of 2020.
